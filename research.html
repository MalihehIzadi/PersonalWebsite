ABOUT ME

I am currently a Postdoctoral Associate in the Software Engineering group (SERG) at Technical University of Delft. I hold a PhD in Software Engineering from Sharif University of Technology, the number one technical university in Iran. My PhD research focused on mining information from version control systems to facilitate software production-related tasks through automatically generating reports for developers and software teams.

I also have a master degree in IT Engineering from SUT, supervised by Dr. Jalili (currently faculty at RMIT University, Melbourne, Australia). My research focus was on improving evaluation methods of recommender systems which resulted in various publications.

In 2020, I visited Software Analytic Lab (SAL), Technical University of Delft, supervised by Dr. Georgios Gousios. We worked on exciting topics including analysing source code using transformer-based techniques to make automated and intelligent solutions for software teams.

Research History and Publication

I am a software engineer with a passion for data science and applied machine learning techniques.  I am an engineer, practitioner, and researcher. I like to direct my research toward fields that are practical to help people in real life. Therefore, I chose the mining software repositories’ field for my Ph.D. thesis to make developers and software managers’ lives easier.

We exploited on the vast amount of data available on GitHub, to find patterns to automatically generate reports and facilitate developers’ and other software team members’ tasks. In our recent Empirical Software Engineering (EMSE) paper (2021), we proposed an approach to automatically recommend topics for software repositories using both classical and SOTA transformer-based multi-label classification techniques. In 2020, we published a paper in the Journal of Systems and Software (JSS), in which we proposed a novel summarization technique using SOTA deep learning techniques and dynamic call graphs for generating explanatory comments, exploiting relationships between methods.

Also, We have worked on improving the quality of users’ answers in the developers Question-Answering (Q&A) website, Stack Overflow, to help other developers program more efficiently. We presented our work at Euromicro SEAA Conference.

For my Msc thesis, I worked on designing novel and useful metrics for evaluation of recommender systems (RS). I presented our conference paper on unifying inconsistent metrics for the specialized workshop of RS evaluation in RecSys ACM Conference, Silicon Valley, 2014. In another paper, we worked on the relationship between two main evaluation metrics of RSs, namely precision vs diversity. This study was published as a book chapter. Finally, due to our experience with RSs, we conducted a comprehensive survey on evaluation of recommender systems. We deployed a large number of collaborative filtering algorithms on various datasets with different contexts, and evaluated them using a wide range of metrics.

I also have worked on other papers and researches, for more information please refer to my Google Scholar profile.
